Mission Briefing

Welcome, rescue team!

Meet Sam. Sam arrived in Sydney two weeks ago and today is Sam's first day at TAFE. But everything has gone wrong! Sam is locked out of the campus, the student ID is missing, the share house key is lost, and a very important phone call is waiting. Your team must help Sam escape six tricky situations using everyday Australian English. Good luck!

How to play

Work as a team of 3 or 4. In each room you must solve a puzzle and complete a speaking task. Some doors need a code from your teacher, so you will need to speak up! You have three hint buttons in each room, but each hint costs your team 2 minutes of time.

🗣️ Before you start: talk together Introduce yourselves to your team. Then agree on a team name and decide who will type. Everyone must speak in every room. That is the deal!
Room 1 of 6

🚧 The Campus Gate

Sam is stuck outside the TAFE gate. The security keypad needs a 4-digit code. A friendly student says: "The code is hidden in the meanings. Match them all and you will see it!"
🗣️ Speaking task first (pairs, 3 minutes) Practise starting a conversation with a stranger, like Min did. Partner A: "Hey! You look a bit lost. Are you new here?" Partner B: reply, introduce yourself, and ask two follow-up questions. Then swap roles. When both of you have practised, start the puzzle.

Puzzle: match each expression to its meaning

Click an expression, then click its meaning. Four of the meanings are hiding a code digit!

Enter the gate code

Room 2 of 6

🪪 The Reception Desk

Sam is inside, but there is a problem. This morning the receptionist walked away without helping, and Sam still has no student ID. Sam must go back and speak up politely. That is not on!

Puzzle: build the polite conversation

The conversation is mixed up. Click the lines in the correct order to build it. If you click a wrong line, it will flash red. Think: how do we complain politely in Australia?

🗣️ Teacher checkpoint: roleplay (pairs) Well done! Now perform the conversation for your teacher without reading every word. Student A is the receptionist. Student B is Sam. Use polite voices and body language. Then swap. When your teacher is happy, they will give you the secret code word.
Room 3 of 6

🏠 The Share House

Sam finally gets home to the share house in Campsie, but the key is missing! The housemates changed the smart lock and left a note: "The password is what good housemates always stick... Complete our messages to find the letters."

Puzzle: complete the housemates' messages

Choose the correct expression for each message. Every correct answer reveals a letter. The letters spell the door password in order, from message 1 to message 8.

🗣️ Speaking task (group, 4 minutes) While you work, take turns to answer out loud: "Tell us about a time you had to sort something out in Australia. What happened? Who helped you?" Everyone speaks for about one minute.

Enter the door password

Room 4 of 6

📞 The Phone Call

Inside the house, Sam's phone rings. It is the TAFE service centre about tomorrow's important appointment. But the phone battery is nearly dead, so only one person can hear the call. The rest of the team must listen carefully and write down the details!
🗣️ Information gap: how it works Choose one team member to be the caller. Only the caller may read the blue card below. The caller reads the message aloud twice, at normal speed. Everyone else looks away from the screen and listens. Listeners can ask checking questions like "Sorry, could you repeat that?" or "Was that Tuesday or Thursday?" Then the listeners complete the form. The caller may not point or help!

☎️ Call script (caller reads this aloud twice)

"Hello, this is the TAFE service centre calling for Sam. This is a reminder about your appointment. Your appointment is on Tuesday at quarter past nine in the morning, at the Bankstown office. Your reference number is four, seven, two, six. Please bring your passport and your enrolment letter. Thank you, and see you then. Goodbye!"

Listeners: complete the appointment form

Room 5 of 6

🛍️ Campsie Shops

Appointment sorted! Now Sam heads to the Campsie shops to buy dinner and meet a friend. But five tricky situations are waiting. Find all five glowing spots in the street, choose the best response, and collect five tokens to unlock the last door.
🗣️ Say it out loud! When your team chooses a response, one person must say it aloud with feeling, like a real Aussie would. Take turns. Your teammates give a thumbs up if it sounds natural.

Room 6 of 6

🎤 The Job-Readiness Workshop

Sam's friend takes the team to a free job-readiness workshop at Campsie Library. There is one final locked door: the workshop stage. The presenter says: "You can only leave when you show us what you have learned. Perform, and the door opens!"

Final challenge: create and perform a conversation

As a team, write and perform a conversation of 2 to 3 minutes. Imagine two new students meeting at TAFE, like Min and Wei. Your conversation must include:

  • at least 6 expressions from today (tick them below as you plan)
  • a friendly opening and a clear closing with a plan to meet again
  • at least one moment of empathy ("I know that feeling", "I totally get that")
  • one checking question ("Are you pulling my leg?", "Is that actually true?")

🗣️ Teacher checkpoint: the performance Practise your conversation twice, then perform it for your teacher (or the whole class). Every team member must speak. When your teacher is happy, they will give you the master code to open the final door.

🎉 YOU ESCAPED! 🎉

Sam has an ID, a house key, an appointment, dinner, and a new group of friends. You are all legends!

Team debrief: talk about these together

  1. Which expression will you use this week? Say it in your own sentence.
  2. Which room was the hardest for your team? Why?
  3. When is it okay to say "That's not on" in real life? Give an example.
  4. What will you do next time someone does not help you at a service desk?
